Product Definition

People that live in a low-income countries,half of the babies born at or below 32 weeks die due to a lack of feasible, cost-effective care, such as warmth, breastfeeding support, and basic care for infections and breathing difficulties.According to the World Health Organization (WHO), An estimated 15 million babies are born too early every year. That is more than 1 in 10 babies. Approximately 1 million children die each year due to complications of preterm birth. Increase in birth rate of premature babies with lower birth weight and other defects is a major factor that drives us to build our neonate incubator. Our main goal is to design and build an efficient and cost effective incubator that works without electricity to meet people that live in a low income countries.
Neonate incubator primarily provide humidity, warmth and oxygen in a controlled environment. The infant incubator is consists of an enclosed chamber, with a small mattress on the top covered by a rigid plastic cover. The chamber protects the baby from noise, dust, and infection. To achieve all these our incubator will be made with wood for the base, and acrylic top with a front door that allows parents to put the neonate inside. Phase Change Material will be used to generate the heat. Heat for its operation can be provided by PCM source.In addition of air blower fan to circulate the air inside the incubator. Once set, the temperature is maintained automatically with the control system which makes the system easy to operate. The system will control it automatically via sensors placed inside the incubator. It is also designed to address unique challenges present in the places it is intended to be used. The market for our product will be mainly for people who live in low-income area. This market has the greatest ability to take advantage of the type of incubator that we will design. In these places, there is a need for incubators and there is no need access to electricity.

Solid-Works of Incubator

Architecture 1

The team come up with two different architectures. The first architecture was considered that the PCM would be the base of the mattress of the baby, where natural air flow would flow inside the incubator and transfer heat with the PCM while inside. The issue with this designs are that the baby would be disturb every time the PCM should be removed from the incubator, and the high efficiency particular air (HEPA) filter would interfered in the air flow. For the filter solution, we discussed the use of an external U.V light that could kill the bacteria of the air that is flowing to the baby area.

Phase change material inside incubator

Architecture 2

The second architecture was considered that our PCM would be outside of the incubator in order to not disturb the baby and do not lose the heat because the incubator would not be opened for heat source re-heat step. The PCM would be inside a box and it would work as a heat exchanger, where copper pipe would flow through the phase change material before flow through the inside of the baby. The cons of this architecture is that there would be need a subsystem to spin a fan so air could go through the pipeline. However, the HEPA filter could be applied once air is forced flowing to the incubator.

Phase change material inside incubator

Engineering Discusion

The design parameters were established upon researching the average size of new born baby, the needs of premature baby, and the standard safety of FDA. Temperature range (36.5 C-kg/for babies in +-30 weeks and 37.2 C-kg/ in +-28 weeks). It is better that the incubator is light in weight so that it can be portable at the same time provides strong support for the components used and can bear the weight of the infant. Isolation of the compartment where the baby is kept from the controlling unit is a necessary requirement. The incubator design can be divided into four subsystems: structural support, enclosure, shell, and bed. The structural support is the device that accommodate the other subsystems and the preterm infant. Design Parameters: Length 40 cm, Height 60 cm, Width 40 cm thickness of material 5mm. Control unit inside the box will have a dimension of 10 cm x 30 cm x 30 cm. The control unit is responsible for holding the heat exchanger and the electrical components needed for the incubator to work. The shell is responsible for retaining the heated air, preventing airborne infections from reaching the preterm infant, and venting the stagnant air. The bed is intended to keep the preterm infant in place. The figure below shows image of the 3D model of incubator structure designed by Computer Aided Design. So,we chose the height ,length and width dimensions based the new born premature baby size which is roughly 16 inches and 3lbs. The normal human body temperature is 37 0C,so the temperature inside the incubator has to be same since we do not know yet baby body temperature.Since it's a premature baby his/her skin is too soft and try quick to avoid the danger we set the humidity from 50-90\% to keep baby skin moisturize.We decide to have 2 portholes and 2 main doors to ease carrying the ICU's and also.It's easy to check on the baby instead open the top which save the baby heat.The main reason we chose to make this incubator lbs 20 because it designs for low-income and villages people, it has to be light and easy to carry around.

Solution and Engineering estimation

We are trying to isolate area inside the environment of incubator with no dust ,bacteria ,and has the ability to control temperature, humidity, and oxygen to remain them in acceptable levels. We design to keep baby warm,to monitor many of their vital body functions like heart rate, blood pressure,oxygen saturation and to support their breathing if necessary. Regarding the temperature and humidity control, incubators should minimize heat loss from the neonate and eddies around him/her.The main physical variables affecting the incubator environment are temperature, humidity,oxygen.
Our mathematical model shows that our main heat losses of the system are conduction and convection n the incubator walls and due radiation. In order to decrease the heat losses of our system we could change our design by changing the material of the walls leaving one or two main walls made by acrylic so there is still visualization of the baby without opening the incubator. Selecting the right material for this project is essential in order to keep it a low cost but still efficient.
Calculations must be made in order to analyse if the peltier and seebeck effect could be applied in order to generate electricity from the difference of temperature of our heat-source and the outside air. Other system discussed was the convertion of potential energy (using mass and height) to rotate the fan. Further discussion would be made with the instructor Dr. Nitsche so the group can come up with the solution.

Manufacturers respond to these demands by introducing double-walled incubators to reduce an infant’s radiated heat loss. Many problems like hypothermia, dehydration will occur in infants if they have less humidity.These problems can be reduced if the preterm infants are nursed at high relative humidity. Also the skin temperature increases and the distribution of surface temperature will be more if an infant is nursed at a high humidity.
